About Me

Computer Science major and Business minor
Building data, AI, and product-driven solutions

I am a Computer Science major with a minor in Business at Michigan State University, where I focus on the intersection of software, data, and strategy. I enjoy turning messy problems into structured solutions, whether that is through code, analytics, or thoughtful product design.

My work spans AI projects, data analytics, and software development. Through MSU’s Global Tech Experience, I have analyzed website engagement data for The Recording Academy/Grammys and explored sustainability data with Intel. I also build my own AI projects using open-source tools and experiment with ways to make models more useful, understandable, and fair.

I created FarmX, a sustainability-focused tool that helps optimize fertilizer use and predict crop yield. This project won the Best Sustainability Track at MHacks, reinforcing my interest in applying AI and analytics to real-world impact. On campus, I am involved with the AI community and continue to grow as a technical problem solver and collaborator.

Beyond tech, I serve as a Resident Assistant at MSU, where I support students, build community, and practice leadership and communication every day. These experiences have shaped how I think about people, systems, and responsibility.

Looking ahead, I plan to pursue an MBA to complement my technical background with business and leadership skills. My long-term goal is to work toward a C-level role where I can guide teams, drive innovation, and build technology that genuinely helps people. When I am not studying or working on a project, you will probably find me playing guitar, recording covers, or planning the next thing I want to build.

A Few Accomplishments

Here are a few projects and experiences that have shaped how I think about technology, people, and impact.

FarmX – Sustainable Agriculture Assistant

FarmX is a tool that helps farmers make smarter fertilizer decisions by predicting crop yield and nitrogen levels using machine learning. Built with Python and deployed via Streamlit, it won the Best Sustainability Track at MHacks and deepened my interest in AI for climate and sustainability.

Global Tech Experience – Recording Academy/Grammys

As part of MSU’s Global Tech Experience, I worked with data from The Recording Academy/Grammys to analyze user behavior and website performance. Using tools like Excel, data visualization, and A/B testing concepts, I explored how data can inform digital strategy and audience engagement.

Global Tech Experience – Intel Sustainability Analytics

In another Global Tech Experience project, I collaborated on sustainability-focused data analysis with Intel. This work involved exploring environmental and operational metrics, building dashboards, and thinking about how data-driven insights can support more sustainable decision-making in large organizations.
